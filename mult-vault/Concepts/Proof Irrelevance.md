# Proof Irrelevance

[[Proof Irrelevance]] is the idea that two proofs of the same proposition are considered indistinguishable.

Example:

```text
p1 : 2 = 2
p2 : 2 = 2
```

Under proof irrelevance, the system cares that `2 = 2` has a proof, not whether the proof was `p1` or `p2`.

This is useful when proofs are meant to justify correctness, not to carry computational data.
